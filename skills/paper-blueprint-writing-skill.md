# paper-blueprint-writing-skill

## Applicable Agent

Writer/Compiler Agent.

## Trigger

Use after research brief, paper pool, evidence pack, and gap analysis are ready, or when revising a prior blueprint after review.

## Input

- Internal research brief.
- Internal paper pool.
- Internal evidence pack.
- Internal gap analysis.
- Reviewer notes when revising.
- User preferences about language, structure, and target output.

## Output

User-facing `paper-blueprint.md`.

## Required Sections

Write a structured blueprint with:

1. Tentative title.
2. One-paragraph project positioning.
3. Abstract structure, not a full final abstract.
4. Introduction logic: problem, research gap, question, contribution.
5. Literature review map: themes, source clusters, and evidence gaps.
6. Research questions and optional hypotheses/propositions.
7. Analytical framework: mechanism, variables, and relationships.
8. Data and variable design: data sources, measurement ideas, missing data risks.
9. Method route: feasible methods, identification limits, robustness checks.
10. Expected contribution: theoretical, empirical, methodological, or policy.
11. Reviewer risks and how to address them.
12. Next writing tasks and next search tasks.

## Writing Rules

1. Preserve the user's research direction and avoid turning the blueprint into a generic article.
2. Use citations or source labels only when they exist in the paper pool or evidence pack.
3. Separate verified evidence from hypotheses and planned claims.
4. Make the blueprint actionable: the user should know what to write or check next.
5. Keep prose concise, academic, and specific.
6. When evidence is thin, explicitly say what must be verified before manuscript writing.

## Citation Handling

- Use DOI, URL, paper ID, or short source labels from the paper pool.
- Do not fabricate APA, MLA, or journal-formatted references from incomplete metadata.
- Maintain a short "Source notes" section if source metadata is incomplete.

## Prohibited Behavior

- Do not write a complete paper manuscript.
- Do not add unverified paper claims.
- Do not hide Reviewer risks.
- Do not cite papers not present in the available context.

## Quality Checks

- Blueprint includes all required sections.
- Every literature claim is source-grounded or explicitly marked as a hypothesis.
- Reviewer concerns are reflected in the risk and method sections.
- The text is useful for continuing real thesis or paper writing.
